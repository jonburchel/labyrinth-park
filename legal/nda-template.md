## RECITALS

The Company possesses certain confidential and proprietary information relating to business plans, property development concepts, architectural designs, technology systems, financial projections, intellectual property (including patent-pending technology), and operational strategies (collectively, "Confidential Information") that the Company wishes to share with the Recipient for the limited purpose of evaluation, discussion, and potential collaboration.

In consideration of the Company's disclosure of such Confidential Information, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"Confidential Information" means all information, whether written, oral, visual, electronic, or in any other form, disclosed by or on behalf of the Company to the Recipient, including without limitation:

(a) Business plans, financial projections, revenue models, budgets, and funding strategies;

(b) Property designs, architectural plans, site layouts, engineering specifications, and construction strategies;

(c) Technology descriptions, patent applications (provisional or otherwise), patent-pending inventions, trade secrets, software designs, algorithms, and system architectures;

(d) Marketing strategies, branding concepts, operational procedures, and customer or guest experience designs;

(e) Entity structures, tax strategies, legal structures, and ownership arrangements;

(f) The identity, role, and involvement of any individual associated with the Company, including but not limited to officers, directors, members, managers, owners, representatives, contractors, and advisors;

(g) Any information marked or identified as "Confidential," "Proprietary," "Patent Pending," or similar designation;

(h) Any information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

## 2. EXCLUSIONS

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Was already known to the Recipient prior to disclosure, as demonstrated by written records predating the disclosure;

(b) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault or action of the Recipient;

(c) Is independently developed by the Recipient without use of or reference to the Confidential Information; or

(d) Is disclosed to the Recipient by a third party who has no obligation of confidentiality to the Company.

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECIPIENT

The Recipient agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;

(b) Not disclose, publish, disseminate, or otherwise communicate any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Company;

(c) Not use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than evaluating and discussing the matters for which it was disclosed;

(d) Not copy, reproduce, photograph, screenshot, summarize, transcribe, or otherwise duplicate any Confidential Information except as expressly authorized in writing by the Company;

(e) Not reverse-engineer, decompile, or otherwise attempt to derive the design or implementation details of any technology, system, or invention described in the Confidential Information;

(f) Limit access to Confidential Information to those individuals within the Recipient's household or immediate professional advisors (attorneys, accountants) who have a need to know and who are bound by obligations of confidentiality at least as restrictive as those in this Agreement;

(g) Promptly notify the Company of any unauthorized disclosure or use of Confidential Information of which the Recipient becomes aware;

(h) Upon request by the Company, promptly return or destroy all copies of Confidential Information in the Recipient's possession, including electronic copies, and certify such return or destruction in writing.

## 4. OWNERSHIP OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

All Confidential Information remains the sole and exclusive property of the Company. No license, right, title, or interest in or to any Confidential Information is granted to the Recipient by this Agreement, whether by implication, estoppel, or otherwise.

## 5. NO OBLIGATION TO PROCEED

The disclosure of Confidential Information does not obligate the Company to proceed with any business transaction, partnership, investment, or other arrangement with the Recipient. The Company reserves the right to discontinue discussions and request return of all Confidential Information at any time.

## 6. TERM AND SURVIVAL

(a) This Agreement is effective as of the date first written above and continues in perpetuity unless terminated by written agreement of both parties.

(b) The Recipient's obligations under Sections 3, 4, 7, and 8 survive any termination of this Agreement and remain in effect indefinitely.

(c) The Recipient acknowledges that the Confidential Information includes trade secrets and patent-pending inventions whose value depends on continued secrecy, and that the obligations of this Agreement are therefore appropriately perpetual.

## 7. LIQUIDATED DAMAGES

The parties acknowledge and agree that:

(a) A breach of this Agreement would cause irreparable harm to the Company that would be difficult or impossible to quantify precisely;

(b) In the event of any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement by the Recipient, the Company shall be entitled to liquidated damages in the amount of **FIVE HUNDRED THOUSAND UNITED STATES DOLLARS ($500,000.00)** per occurrence of breach, in addition to (and not in lieu of) any other remedies available at law or in equity;

(c) The parties agree that this liquidated damages amount is a reasonable estimate of the anticipated harm from a breach, and is not intended as a penalty;

(d) Each separate act of unauthorized disclosure, use, or reproduction constitutes a separate occurrence of breach for purposes of this Section;

(e) The Recipient has read and understands this provision and has had the opportunity to seek independent legal counsel regarding its effect.

## 8. INJUNCTIVE RELIEF

The Recipient acknowledges that any breach of this Agreement may cause immediate and irreparable injury to the Company for which monetary damages alone would be inadequate. The Company shall therefore be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, and any other equitable remedies, without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting a bond, in addition to all other remedies available at law.

## 9. IDENTITY OF COMPANY PRINCIPALS

The Recipient specifically acknowledges and agrees that:

(a) The identity of the owners, members, managers, and principals of the Company constitutes Confidential Information of the highest sensitivity;

(b) The Recipient shall not disclose, discuss, speculate about, or attempt to discover the identity of any such individuals to or with any third party;

(c) Any disclosure of such identity, whether confirmed or speculative, shall constitute a material breach of this Agreement subject to the liquidated damages provision of Section 7.

## 10. NON-DISPARAGEMENT

The Recipient shall not make any disparaging, defamatory, or negative statements about the Company, its business plans, its technology, its properties, or any individual associated with the Company, whether publicly or privately, during or after the term of this Agreement.

## 11. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Wyoming, without regard to conflicts of law principles. Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Wyoming, and the parties hereby consent to the personal jurisdiction of such courts.

## 12. ENTIRE AGREEMENT

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements, whether written or oral. This Agreement may not be modified except by a written instrument signed by both parties.

## 13. SEVERABILITY

If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect, and the invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable.

## 14. WAIVER

No failure or delay by the Company in exercising any right under this Agreement shall operate as a waiver of that right. A waiver of any breach shall not constitute a waiver of any subsequent breach.

## 15. ACKNOWLEDGMENT

The Recipient acknowledges that:

(a) The Recipient has read this Agreement in its entirety and understands all of its terms;

(b) The Recipient has had the opportunity to seek independent legal counsel before signing;

(c) The Recipient is signing this Agreement voluntarily and without coercion;

(d) The Recipient understands that breach of this Agreement may result in liquidated damages of **$500,000.00 per occurrence** plus injunctive relief and attorney's fees.
