## 0. Mission & Philosophy

> Labyrinth Park is not a theme park. It is not a facade. It is a genuine labyrinth: mysterious, ever-shifting, never fully known.

> The park is a real expression of its creator. Jon wanted to be like a spy, with tunnels and security and hidden passages. But he is not a spy, and pretending to be one felt hollow. With Labyrinth Park, the mystery is not faked. He IS a genuinely mysterious person, who DOES live in a fortress, about which nobody knows very much, and from which exude bizarre wonders that nobody imagined. But he is not false at all. He is exactly what he is, and nothing more or less.

> His home is not a facade. It is a real expression of him, and the very real mystery and labyrinth that IS him.

### What Labyrinth Park Is
- A **home**, first and foremost, with its own magnificent parks
- A **hotel** to serve guests when the home overflows
- A **business** that makes the vision financially possible
- A **contribution to the community**, not reclusive consumption of it
- A **living labyrinth**: mysterious, ever-shifting, never fully known, perhaps even with some element of danger worked into a positive
- An experience where visitors only ever see a fraction of what's really happening

### What Labyrinth Park Is NOT
- A theme park (no rides, no characters, no mass tourism)
- A Disney mockup of a labyrinth (though we take inspiration from Disney's design discipline)
- A facade or performance of mystery
- Open to the general public on demand
- Ever fully explorable, even by the most dedicated visitor

### Design Principles
- **Genuine over performed.** Every mystery is real. The tunnels are real tunnels. The hidden passages lead to real places. The archaeology is conducted by real universities.
- **Scarcity over abundance.** Limited admissions, sections that open and close. The complete experience is a moving target that can never be captured.
- **Discovery over presentation.** Guests find things; they are not shown things. The wishing well, the whispering gallery, the fog garden: you stumble into them.
- **Community over consumption.** The hotel creates jobs and revenue. The nonprofits serve real missions. The university partnerships produce real research. The farm feeds real people.
- **Disney discipline, not Disney culture.** We borrow Imagineering's spatial compression, sight-line management, and world-building rigor. We do not borrow their mass-market, IP-driven, throughput-optimized mentality.

### The Denial Doctrine

**Labyrinth Park never confirms, never advertises, and never explains the following:**
- That the maze reconfigures itself
- That artifacts are placed by staff
- The existence or nature of the tunnel network
- The identity of the owner
- The age or origin of the maze
- How any specific section is accessed

**Official position on all of the above:** "We've heard these reports. Some aspects of the property continue to surprise us. We don't have a complete understanding of the property's full history or behavior."

**Staff NDA (critical):**
- Employment agreement includes perpetual non-disclosure of operational details
- Staff may NOT confirm or deny that paths change, that artifacts are placed, or how any mechanical system works
- Staff MAY say: "I've heard guests mention that," "It does seem different sometimes," "We're still learning about the property ourselves"
- Staff may NOT say anything that confirms deliberate human engineering of any mystery
- Violation: liquidated damages (specific dollar amount), surviving termination indefinitely
- Standard for themed entertainment (Disney cast members sign equivalent agreements)

**The patent is not a threat to the mystery:**
- Provisional patents are completely confidential (never published)
- Full utility patent publishes ~2.5 years after provisional filing, long after the legend is established
- A patent is a dry technical document; nobody searching "Labyrinth Park mystery" finds USPTO filings
- If a Reddit detective eventually finds it, it deepens the conspiracy: "THEY HAVE A PATENT. WHAT ELSE ARE THEY HIDING?"

**Marketing approach: cultivate a legend, not a brand.**
- Year 1: locals notice, post on Facebook groups
- Year 2: regional travel bloggers write "The Strangest Park in NC"
- Year 3: Reddit thread goes viral
- Year 4: Atlas Obscura, then NYT travel section
- Year 5+: the internet has done the marketing; official response is still "We've heard these reports"
- Strategic anonymous social media posts to catalyze discovery (never traceable to the organization)
- Legends don't have press releases

## 7. The Labyrinth & Hedge System

### Vision
- **4 miles of walkable hedge maze** connecting 7-10 unique themed worlds
- Dozens of surprises and little secrets
- Property surrounded by 13' hedge, appearing as a single massive hedge maze
- Amenity areas: convenient, easy to navigate
- Challenge sections: very twisty, intentionally hard to navigate
- Several miles of curated woodland trails within the 30 acres
- Open tunnel sections styled as old stone bridges with tunnels underneath

### Hedge Metrics
- **Perimeter hedge (30-acre boundary):** ~4,800 LF (0.91 miles)
- **Internal maze (4 mi walkable path):** ~8 miles of hedge rows (both sides)
- **Total hedge needed:** ~8.9 miles (~47,000 LF)
- **Maze density:** ~3,000-3,800 LF of walkable path per acre at standard density
- **Acres of pure maze needed:** ~6-8 acres for 4 miles of path
- **Hedge footprint:** ~2.9 acres of ground covered by hedge alone

### Hedge Species (5 Tiers)

| Tier | Species | Size | Cost/Mile | $/LF | Time to 13ft | Best Use |
|------|---------|------|-----------|------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Green Giant Arborvitae | 5-6ft | $121K | $23 | 3-4 yr | Bulk maze corridors |
| 2 | Nellie R. Stevens Holly | 6-8ft B&B | $380K | $72 | 4-6 yr | Perimeter, security |
| 3 | European Beech / Am. Holly | 8-10ft B&B | $1.32M | $249 | 3-5 yr | Featured formal areas |
| 4 | Green Giant / Nellie Stevens | 12-14ft specimen | $1.58M | $299 | Immediate | Instant impact zones |
| 5 | English Yew / Eu. Beech specimen | 12-13ft imported | $6.68M | $1,265 | Immediate | Signature gardens |

### Species Notes
- **Green Giant Arborvitae:** Fastest grower (3-5 ft/yr). Lifespan 40-60yr. Disease risk in humid SE. Not formally shearable. Best as "scaffolding" hedge, replaced over time with formal species.
- **Nellie R. Stevens Holly:** Workhorse SE hedge. Native-adapted, evergreen, dense to ground level, impenetrable when mature. 6-10ft thick at maturity. Drought tolerant. 80-100+ yr lifespan. Spiny leaves deter entry. THE perimeter hedge.
- **European Beech:** Retains copper-brown dead leaves through winter (marcescent) for year-round opacity. THE classic English manor hedge. Lives 200+ years. Needs good drainage.
- **English Yew:** Lives 500+ years. Versailles quality. Marginal in NC heat/humidity; needs shade and drainage. Special order, 1-2yr lead time.

### Perimeter Hedge Recommendation
- **Double-stagger row** of Nellie Stevens Holly (two rows offset 3ft apart, zigzag)
- Eliminates line-of-sight gaps from day one
- Creates 9-10ft thick wall at maturity
- ~60% more cost but fortress-grade from planting
- Optional: temporary black welded-wire fence ($3-5/LF) down center line during establishment, swallowed by hedge in 2-3 years

- Numerous side tunnels interconnecting spokes
- Extend special-purpose passages

### Four Functional Networks (Overlaid)
1. **Public spectacle (~10-15% of footage):** Stone bridge crossings, decorative tunnel moments. Themed, lit, photographable.
2. **Donor experience (~5%):** Curated behind-the-scenes tours. "Let me show you something most people never see." Tiny fragment of the real network.
3. **Service/utility backbone (~70%):** Power, water, fiber, HVAC, staff movement, golf cart logistics. Functional, well-lit, not pretty.
4. **Owner's private network (~10-15%):** Finely finished residential corridors. Beautiful, intimate, invisible. Known in full to exactly one person.

### Construction Approach
- **Shallow excavation:** 4-6' down into soft clay (stays above water table at 800ft elevation)
- **Precast concrete sections** laid in trench
- **Spoil as fill:** Excavated dirt becomes backfill along sides/top of concrete AND the material for artificial hills and berming
- Soil is self-supporting, no shoring needed
- Earth-sheltered / bermed architecture: buildings emerge from artificial hills

### Hotel-to-Residence Tunnels
- Finely finished like residential hallways
- Artwork, beautiful lighting
- Walking from hotel suite to main house feels like passing through a wing of the same house
- Actually passes under a bermed hill and high wall
- Distance calibrated: close enough for a pleasant stroll, no golf cart needed

- **Primary (known to intimates):** House to guesthouse, house to rose garden, house to wall exit
- **Secondary private egress:** House to beyond exterior wall

- All on unified slab
- Vehicle tunnels: rectangular precast box culverts (10-12ft clear residential, 14ft clear service)
- Pedestrian tunnels: 6ft diameter round precast

- Hidden tunnel exits in each of 3 maze regions + 1 exterior emergency egress

- Total: ~300-400 LF residential pedestrian plus vehicle entry tunnel (earlier estimate, now superseded by 5,350+ LF main network)

## 9. Arrival Sequence & Stations

### Hotel Arrival Labyrinth (Reconfigurable)
- **Concept:** The drive into the hotel passes through a reconfigurable hedge labyrinth that changes nightly
- **Path length:** ~0.25 miles (1,320 ft), approximately 60 seconds at 15 mph
- **Region:** ~3 acres, compact Z-shaped pattern within hotel allocation
- **Hedge walls:** 8-10 ft tall (shorter than park maze, immersive from driver's seat)
- **Lane width:** 12 ft one-way with hedge walls on both sides
- **Junction points:** 8-10 movable hedge sections using the patented trough-and-elevator system
- **Valid routes:** 38-307+ unique configurations (one per day for a year without repeating)
- **Rotation:** New route nightly (reconfigured after last arrival, before first morning departure)
- **Bridge crossover:** At least one point where the road dips ~8 ft below grade, passing under a hedge-walled bridge carrying another route segment overhead. Creates dramatic descent/ascent experience and enables routes to cross without intersection.
- **Guest experience:** Returning guests never drive the same route twice. Guests at dinner compare notes: "You went UNDER the bridge? We went OVER it." The hotel breathes labyrinth from the first moment of arrival.
- **Patent coverage:** Same movable hedge system (Sections 2-3 of provisional patent). Vehicular-scale application.

**1. Main Guest Entrance:**
- Gate (flush cattle grid, no columns)

**2. Service/Guesthouse Entrance:**

**3. Owner's Entrance:**

## 21B. The Labyrinth Park Archaeological Trust & Treasure System

- Budget: ~$50K/year amortized

## 24. Tax Strategy

### Roth Conversion Strategy (Pre-Move, FL Residency)
- Withdraw $475-520K/year from pre-tax 401(k) while FL resident (no state income tax)
- Stay within 24% federal bracket (taxable income <= $201,775 after deductions)
- NC state tax saved: ~$20K+/year x 3 years = ~$60K+ preserved
- Convert withdrawn funds to Roth IRA after taxes and living expenses
- Target: $1.4-1.6M into Roth over 3 years at 24% marginal rate
- Alternative aggressive: $2M/year conversions, hitting 35-37% bracket, ~$3.9M into Roth over 3 years

### Business Loss Shelter (During Build-Out)
- 100% bonus depreciation (permanent under OBBBA) on land improvements placed in service:
  - 15-year property: paths, landscaping, fencing, lighting, irrigation, drainage, parking, hedge maze hardscaping
  - 5-7 year property: benches, signage, fixtures, equipment, spa equipment, kitchen equipment
  - NOT eligible: land itself, building structures (39-year), Inn building shell
- Fund construction via loans (not personal cash); depreciation still applies
- Loan interest deductible as business expense (§163)
- At-risk rules (§465): loans must be recourse/personally guaranteed
- §461(l) excess business loss cap: ~$256K/year (single filer, 2026 OBBBA clawback)
  - Losses exceeding cap become NOL carryforward (usable at 80% of future taxable income)
- Cost segregation study on Inn: reclassify 30-40% of $12-15M build into accelerated categories = $3.6-6M in bonus-eligible deductions

### SBA 504 + Depreciation Synergy
- SBA 504 equity of $1.5-2.5M covered entirely by bonus depreciation deductions
- Pulls taxable income well below RMD crunch threshold
- Net after-tax cost of equity approaches zero

### Conservation Easement (Year 3-4)
- Qualified conservation easement on undeveloped horse farm acreage (~20-30 acres)
- Charitable deduction for reduction in property value from permanent restriction
- Up to 50% of AGI (100% if qualified farmer/rancher), 15-year carryforward
- Must use qualified land trust
- Permanently restricts development on those acres

### Steady-State Retirement (Post Build-Out)
- NC resident, itemizing: mortgage interest (~$40-45K) + SALT (NC income + property tax, capped $40,400) + charitable (~$5K) = ~$65-68K itemized deductions
- Max gross income in 24% bracket: ~$268-275K/year
- Most income from Roth (tax-free), supplemented by minimal 401(k) draws
- QBI deduction (20%) on net business income from park/Inn once profitable
- Park operating expenses deductible against business revenue

### Key Tax Numbers (2026, Single Filer)

## 28. Construction & Precast Strategy

### Precast Concrete as Unified Building System
- Standardize on precast across entire property: tunnels, perimeter walls, building shells
- **Regional manufacturers:** Tindall (Spartanburg SC), Metromont, Smith-Midland (all within hours of Browns Summit)
- Volume discount: 15-25% likely by consolidating into single phased contract
- Total precast: ~10,650+ LF (tunnels + perimeter wall + building shells)

### Precast Cost Reference

| Use Case | Cost/LF |
|----------|---------|
| Above-grade building shell (25x15) | $600-900 |
| Below-grade tunnel (with excavation, waterproofing) | $2,000-4,000 |
| Hybrid earth-bermed | $1,200-2,000 |
| Perimeter wall panels | Included in wall budget |

### Stacking
- Precast sections routinely stacked 2-3 levels (parking garages, warehouses, industrial)
- Joints: grouted shear keys, welded steel connections, or post-tensioned cables
- For buildings: use precast wall panels + hollow-core floor slabs + double-tee roof sections
- Tunnel roof designed as structural slab for overburden (3-6ft earth = 400-800 psf, standard capacity)

### Earth-Sheltered Construction
- Tunnel roof as foundation platform for structures above
- Excavated spoil piled on top and around to create artificial hills
- Buildings emerge from hills at various levels
- Guests see rolling hills with nestled buildings; no awareness of tunnel grid underneath
- Same principles as Hobbiton, modern earth-sheltered homes, military installations
