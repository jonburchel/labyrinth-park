- Operates park (Sunday/holiday public admission, events, weddings)
- Operates or leases The Inn at The Labyrinth (16-suite luxury hotel)
- Collects fair-market rent from charitable tenants
- Files Schedule C (or partnership return if structured as multi-member LLC)

### Lisa's Horse Charity – 501(c)(3)
- Independent board (Lisa as ED, independent majority required)
- Mission: therapeutic/educational equine programs for underserved youth
- Leases 30-acre horse farm from LLC at fair market rate
- Separately boards horses for paying clients (LLC revenue or charity UBIT – structure TBD)
- Primary beneficiary of annual fundraising gala

### Education/Conservation Nonprofit – 501(c)(3)
- Runs programs at the park: botanical education, school field trips, conservation, veteran/senior programs
- Pays LLC fair-market facility-use fee for park access during program hours
- Independent governance required

### Key Legal Constraints
- §4941: Cannot sell property to own private foundation (self-dealing)
- §501(c)(3): Cannot maintain family control of nonprofit assets; nonprofits must serve public purpose
- Solution: LLC retains ownership; nonprofits are arm's-length tenants with genuine charitable missions
- Wedding/event revenue in a nonprofit context = UBIT; keep commercial activities in LLC

## 3. Tax Strategy

### Roth Conversion Strategy (Pre-Move, FL Residency)
- Withdraw $475-520K/year from pre-tax 401(k) while FL resident (no state income tax)
- Stay within 24% federal bracket (taxable income ≤ $201,775 after deductions)
- NC state tax saved: ~$20K+/year × 3 years = ~$60K+ preserved
- Convert withdrawn funds to Roth IRA after taxes and living expenses
- Target: $1.4-1.6M into Roth over 3 years at 24% marginal rate
- Alternative aggressive approach: $2M/year conversions, hitting 35-37% bracket, ~$3.9M into Roth over 3 years

### Business Loss Shelter (During Build-Out)
- 100% bonus depreciation (permanent under OBBBA) on land improvements placed in service:
  - 15-year property: paths, landscaping, fencing, lighting, irrigation, drainage, parking, hedge maze hardscaping
  - 5-7 year property: benches, signage, fixtures, equipment, spa equipment, kitchen equipment
  - NOT eligible: land itself, building structures (39-year), Inn building shell
- Fund construction via loans (not personal cash) – depreciation still applies
- Loan interest deductible as business expense (§163)
- At-risk rules (§465): loans must be recourse/personally guaranteed
- §461(l) excess business loss cap: ~$256K/year (single filer, 2026 OBBBA clawback)
  - Losses exceeding cap become NOL carryforward (usable at 80% of future taxable income)
- Cost segregation study on Inn: reclassify 30-40% of $12-15M build into accelerated categories = $3.6-6M in bonus-eligible deductions

### Conservation Easement (Year 3-4)
- Qualified conservation easement on undeveloped horse farm acreage (~20-30 acres)
- Charitable deduction for reduction in property value from permanent restriction
- Up to 50% of AGI (100% if qualified farmer/rancher), 15-year carryforward
- Must use qualified land trust; IRS aggressive on syndicated easements but legitimate personal easements defensible
- Permanently restricts development on those acres

### Steady-State Retirement (Post Build-Out)
- NC resident, itemizing: mortgage interest (~$40-45K) + SALT (NC income tax + property tax, capped $40,400) + charitable (~$5K) = ~$65-68K itemized deductions
- Max gross income in 24% bracket: ~$268-275K/year
- Most income from Roth (tax-free), supplemented by minimal 401(k) draws
- QBI deduction (20%) on net business income from park/Inn once profitable
- Park operating expenses (maintenance, insurance, groundskeeping, staff) deductible against business revenue

### Key Tax Numbers (2026, Single Filer)
- Standard deduction: $16,100
- SALT cap: $40,400 (phasedown at $505K MAGI, 30% reduction rate)
- 24% bracket ceiling: $201,775 taxable income
- 32% bracket ceiling: $256,225 taxable income
- 35% bracket starts: $256,226

- Secondary (60-90 min): Raleigh-Durham (2.2M), Charlotte (2.7M)

- Nearest comparable: Barnsley Resort (rural Georgia, 3,000 acres, $350-$800+/night, strong occupancy) – 4 hours south in arguably weaker market

### Strengths

### Weaknesses
- No brand recognition initially; building story from scratch
- No "town" nearby – property must be fully self-contained
- Seasonality: spring/fall strong, summer hot, winter quiet
